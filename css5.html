CSS Introduction

Border Styles:

Sample Program :

<!Doctype Html >
<html >
<head >
<style >
p.dotted {border-style:dotted;} p.dashed {border-style:dashed;} p.solid {border-style:solid;} p.double {border-style:doubled;} p.groove {border-style:groove;} p.ridge {border-style:ridge;} p.inset {border-style:inset;} p.outset {border-style:outset;} p.none {border-style:none;} p.hidden {border-style:hidden;} p.mix {border-style:mix;} </style >
</head >
<body>
<h2>The border-style Property:</h2>
<p>This property specifies what kind of border to display:</p>
<p class="dotted">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="dashed">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="solid">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="double">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="groove">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="ridge">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="inset">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="outset">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="none">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="hidden">A dotted border.</p>
<p class="mix">A dotted border.</p>
</body>
</html>

Out Put:

The border-style Property

This property specifies what kind of border to display:

A dotted border.

A dashed border.

A solid border.

A double border.

A groove border.

A ridge border.

An inset border.

An outset border.

No border.

A hidden border.

A mixed border.
